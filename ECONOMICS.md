# Economics Analysis

## What Is a Converted Lead Worth?

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits to startups already spending heavily on AI tools.

A converted customer could realistically generate:

- $2,000–$20,000 annually in infrastructure credit purchases
- recurring SaaS optimization opportunities
- long-term enterprise relationships

Assuming:
- average converted customer value = $5,000/year
- gross margin ≈ 20%

each converted customer could generate approximately:
- $1,000 gross profit annually

# Funnel Assumptions

## Example Funnel

10,000 visitors  
↓  
2,000 completed audits (20%)  
↓  
300 consultation requests (15%)  
↓  
60 paying customers (20%)  

This results in:
- 0.6% visitor-to-customer conversion rate

At:
- $5,000 average customer value

Revenue:
- 60 × $5,000
= $300,000 ARR

# Why This Can Become Profitable

The economics improve because:
- the audit tool itself is inexpensive to operate
- users self-qualify based on spending
- high-savings startups naturally become high-value leads

The audit effectively becomes a lead-generation funnel disguised as a free utility.

# What Must Be True To Reach $1M ARR

To reach $1M ARR within 18 months:

Assume:
- average customer value = $5,000/year

Required customers:
- 200 active customers

Possible funnel:
- 100,000 total audit users
- 5,000 qualified leads
- 1,000 consultations
- 200 conversions

This is realistic if:
- the tool gains strong organic distribution
- screenshots become shareable
- benchmark insights create repeat usage
- Credex converts enterprise AI spenders effectively

---

# Major Risks

## Low User Trust
If recommendations feel inaccurate, users will ignore savings suggestions.

## Weak Distribution
Without strong organic sharing, acquisition costs could become expensive.

## Pricing Volatility
AI pricing changes rapidly, so stale recommendations could reduce product credibility.

---

# Why The Model Still Works

Even modest conversion rates can produce strong economics because:
- infrastructure customers are high-value
- operational savings are easy to demonstrate
- AI tooling spend is growing rapidly
- founders actively seek cost optimization
