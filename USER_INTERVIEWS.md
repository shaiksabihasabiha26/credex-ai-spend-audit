# User Interviews

## Interview 1

### Person
A.K — Engineering Lead at a 12-person SaaS startup

### Quotes
- “We started buying AI tools individually before finance even tracked them.”
- “Cursor and ChatGPT subscriptions multiplied faster than expected.”
- “Nobody on our team actually knows whether we’re overspending.”

### Most Surprising Insight
The company had multiple overlapping AI subscriptions with no centralized visibility.

### What It Changed
I prioritized making savings visibility extremely obvious on the dashboard.

## Interview 2

### Person
R.M — Founder of a small AI automation startup

### Quotes
- “I pay for multiple AI products because I’m scared of losing productivity.”
- “I honestly don’t compare plans often.”
- “I’d definitely use a free optimization tool before talking to sales.”

### Most Surprising Insight
Fear of productivity loss was driving unnecessary software spending.

### What It Changed
I added softer recommendation messaging instead of aggressive “cut costs” language.

## Interview 3

### Person
S.P — Freelance developer building side projects

### Quotes
- “I switch between Claude and ChatGPT constantly.”
- “Monthly AI spending creeps up quietly.”
- “A benchmark against other developers would actually be useful.”

### Most Surprising Insight
Benchmark comparisons felt more valuable to users than raw savings numbers alone.

### What It Changed
It reinforced the idea of adding benchmark analytics in future iterations.
